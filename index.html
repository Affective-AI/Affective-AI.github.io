Introduction

Vision-Language Models (VLMs) have demonstrated strong performance on perception and reasoning tasks, yet their capacity to analyze affective visual content remains underexplored. To address this gap, we introduce AICA-VLM, a unified benchmark for evaluating the emotional intelligence of VLMs through Affective Image Content Analysis (AICA). AICA-VLM comprises three core tasks: emotion understanding (EU), emotion reasoning (ER), and emotion-guided content generation (ECG), spanning 9 diverse datasets. We construct 18,124 unique instructions, each offered in both base and chain-of-thought (CoT) prompting versions, to support systematic multi-task evaluation. We benchmark 23 open- and closed-source VLMs under zero-shot settings, and conduct detailed analyses across tasks, prompting strategies, and model types. Furthermore, we introduce an automatic scoring model, fine-tuned from Qwen2.5VL-7B on 2,000 human-annotated samples, to evaluate open-ended affective responses in ER and ECG.

Benchmark Framework


The AICA-VLM benchmark consists of three core modules: (1) AICA Tasks, which defines affective tasks and instruction prompts; (2) Model Inference, which handles inference across open- and closed-source VLMs; and (3) the Evaluation Module, which supports both automatic and learned scoring for multiple- and open-ended tasks.

Leaderboard

Quantitative results for 23 Vision-Language Models (VLMs) across 11 AICA tasks are presented. All scores are reported as percentages. Tasks are grouped into three categories: Emotion Understanding (EU), Emotion Reasoning (ER), and Emotion-guided Content Generation (ECG). For each task, the average score across relevant subtasks is computed (e.g., EU Avg., ER Avg., EG Avg.), and a final Overall Avg. score is calculated across all categories. The best-performing value for each task column is highlighted in bold. Models are further categorized by source type (Closed-source vs. Open-source), distinguished by row background color. Note: While some open-source models support advanced capabilities such as multi-image input or instruction tuning, their training data or task exposure may vary significantly. Full task definitions and abbreviations (e.g., ER EA, EG Desc.) are provided in the paper for reference.
